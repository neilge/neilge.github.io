This is a summary of the words and phrases that I come cross in the daily life, especially in TV series and books.

Word

  1. inkling: a slight knowledge or suspicion; a hint.
  2. suspicion: a feeling or thought that something is possible, likely, or true.
  3. incorporate: take in or contain (something) as part of a whole; include.
  4. aside: a remark that is not directly related to the main topic of discussion.
  5. deliberate: done consciously and intentionally.
  6. caveat: a warning or proviso of specific stipulation, condition or limitation
  1. provoke: stimulate or give rise to (a reaction or emotion, typically a strong or unwelcome one) in someone.
  2. tilde: ~
  3. compliment: a polite expression of praise or admiration.
  4. benchmark: a standard or point of reference against which things may be compared or assessed.
  5. tension: the state of being stretched tight.
  6. vengeance: revenge
  7. conviction: a formal declaration that someone is guilty of a criminal offense, made by the verdict of a jury or the decision of a judge in a court of law.
  8. jeopardize: put (someone or something) into a situation in which there is a danger of loss, harm, or failure.
  9. neologism: a newly coined word or expression.
  10. hostile: unfriendly; antagonistic.
  11. boilerplate: refers to sections of code that have to be included in many places with little or no alteration. verbose
  12. obedience: compliance with an order, request, or law or submission to another’s authority.
  13. mediocre: of only moderate quality; not very good.
  14. shred: tear or cut into shreds.
  15. whatsoever: at all (used for emphasis)
  16. sluggish: slow-moving or inactive.
  17. motivation
  18. cardio: cardiovascular exercise.
  19. anaerobic
  20. sarcasm: the use of irony to mock or convey contempt.
  21. contempt: the feeling that a person or a thing is beneath consideration, worthless, or deserving scorn.
  22. dispicable
  23. regime
  24. shot
  25. desert: abadon in a way considered disloyal or treacherous
  26. waistline
  27. radical
  28. obsolete: no longer produced or used; out of date.
